# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual trip planners researching personal travel — weekends, getaways, anniversaries. They open Waypoint after work, on the couch, with a destination already in mind. They want a curated plan handed to them by someone who knows what they're doing, not a search results page to sift through.

## Product Purpose

Waypoint is an AI travel planner that takes a trip description and returns a complete, opinionated plan: flights (economy and first class), hotels across budget tiers, and a day-by-day itinerary. It replaces the 2-hour research session with a single smart answer.

## Brand Personality

Warm, confident, direct. A well-traveled friend who gives you real picks and skips the hedging. Not corporate, not a data tool, not a marketplace. The interface feels like getting a text from someone who's already done all the research.

Three words: knowledgeable, personal, unhurried.

## Anti-references

- Airbnb / Booking.com — photo-heavy marketplace, listing grids, review counts, price comparison tables. Waypoint gives you a plan, not a marketplace.
- Google Flights / Kayak — dense, tabular, search-engine aesthetic. No personality.
- Generic AI startup — cream backgrounds, gradient text, eyebrow labels, hero metrics.

## Design Principles

1. **Give the answer** — the output is the product. The form disappears once results are in. The plan takes the whole stage.
2. **Opinionated, not exhaustive** — surface the best options with a recommendation, not a list of 20. The model did the work; the UI should reflect that confidence.
3. **Dark by default** — this is late-evening, post-work, planning-on-the-couch software. The dark theme isn't aesthetic decoration; it matches the context of use.
4. **Warmth in the brand, not the surface** — the green and amber carry personality. The background is near-black; warmth comes from color and copy, not a sand-tinted bg.
5. **Motion earns its place** — results appear with intention. Loading feels like thinking, not spinning.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Reduced-motion support on all transitions.
